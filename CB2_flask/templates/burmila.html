Name Burmilla (a mix of Burmese and Chinchilla Persian breeds)
Size Medium-sized
Weight: 6–13 lbs (2.7–5.9 kg)
Length: 10–14 inches
Lifespan 10-15 years (some live longer with good care)
Temperament Affectionate & Loving – Enjoys human companionship, loves to cuddle
Playful & Energetic – Active but not overly demanding
Social & Friendly – Gets along well with families, other pets, and children
Curious & Intelligent – Quick learners, enjoy interactive play
Independent Yet Loyal – Loves company but can entertain itself
Color and Appearance Short to medium-length coat (soft, silky texture)
Silver or shaded coat (with black, blue, chocolate, lilac, caramel)
Striking green eyes (one of their most unique features)
Child Friendly Burmillas are gentle, patient, and playful, making them great for families.
Health and Survival Issues Polycystic Kidney Disease (PKD) (inherited from Persian ancestry)
Respiratory issues (due to their short muzzle)
Obesity (monitor diet and exercise)
Trainability Highly trainable – Can learn tricks, commands, and even walk on a leash. They respond well to interactive play and positive reinforcement.
Overview The Burmilla is a rare and beautiful cat breed known for its sweet personality, playful energy, and striking silver coat. They make excellent companions for families, singles, and multi-pet households.
