Topic Details
Name Blanc de Hotot Rabbit
Size Medium to Large
Weight: 8–11 lbs (3.6–5 kg)
Lifespan 7-10 years (with proper care)
Temperament Gentle & Friendly – Enjoys human interaction and being handled
Calm & Affectionate – Makes a great companion for families
Curious & Playful – Loves to explore and play with toys
Sociable but Reserved – Bonds well with owners but may be shy at first
Color and Appearance Pure white fur with distinct black rings around the eyes, giving a “mascara” effect
Dense, soft coat that requires regular grooming
Compact, well-muscled body with upright ears
Child Friendly Yes, with supervision! They are gentle and affectionate, making them great for families, but young kids should be taught to handle them carefully.
Common Health Issues Overgrown Teeth – Needs plenty of hay and chew toys
GI Stasis – A serious digestive issue if they stop eating
Obesity – Needs a proper diet and exercise to stay healthy
Eye Infections – Due to their distinctive eye markings, they may be more prone to irritation
Trainability Highly trainable – Can learn to use a litter box, respond to their name, and perform simple tricks with positive reinforcement.
Overview The Blanc de Hotot is a beautiful, affectionate rabbit breed known for its unique eye markings, friendly nature, and calm personality. Originally bred in France, they make great pets for families and individuals. With proper care, space to hop around, and a balanced diet, they can be loving, long-term companions.
