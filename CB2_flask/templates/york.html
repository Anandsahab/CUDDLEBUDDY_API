Name York Chocolate (also called York or Yorkie Cat)
Size Medium to Large
Weight: 10–16 lbs (4.5–7.2 kg)
Length: 12–18 inches
Lifespan 13-15 years
Temperament Affectionate & Loyal – Forms strong bonds with owners, loves attention
Playful & Energetic – Enjoys playing but also loves relaxing near its owner
Intelligent – Quick learners, interested in exploring their surroundings
Social – Friendly with family members but may be shy around strangers
Vocal & Expressive – Communicates with soft meows and purrs
Color and Appearance Chocolate-brown or lavender-brown coat (rich, silky texture)
Semi-long fur (requires moderate grooming)
Golden, green, or hazel eyes
Child Friendly York Chocolate cats are patient and playful, making them good family pets.
Health and Survival Issues Generally a healthy breed with no known major genetic disorders
Obesity can be a concern, so a balanced diet and exercise are essential
Regular dental care is recommended
Trainability Highly trainable – Can learn commands, tricks, and even how to walk on a leash. They are intelligent and respond well to positive reinforcement.
Overview The York Chocolate is a rare and elegant cat breed known for its rich brown coat, affectionate nature, and playful personality. Originally bred in the 1980s in the U.S., this breed is now quite rare. They make excellent pets for families and individuals who want a loving, social, and intelligent cat.
